#BACKGROUND

>>> THE START Middle School, 2003

Enamored by the way anything works, the draw to development was a natural course for me in my adolescance, especially after receiving my own computer at age 12. Always the painter, I explored the digital side making raster images with Jasc Paint Shop Pro 8 & GIFs (frame by frame!) with Animation Shop. My curiosity grew, and it wasn't long until I was learning then-HTML3 on Windows NotePads creating pages for my virtual pet websites, soon followed by XHTML, CSS, JavaScript.

A hiatus fell in after my first year of high school. When I picked up developing again, it was again visually inspired, but this time lending to Flash programming, specifically Action- & Dynamic-Script. I learned through making vector images, creating animated, interactive pictures (think Harry Potter), and by making games, applications, and websites.

>>> CONTINUING Seeking formal instruction

Again with the demand of higher education, I fell out of development & programming my first year of university. I continued to design when I could, but while "coding" waned, the interest never did.

It wasn't until 2017 when I was able to put my life on hold to pivot towards formal instruction with the post-graduate program, Covalence, firmly committing to become a developer.
