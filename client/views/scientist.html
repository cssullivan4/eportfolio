#INTRODUCTION

Brief Background

I was born the 4th child of 5 to grandchildren of immigrants with a strong family culture. My mother was a counseling psychologist in a mental health hospital working on severe, terminal cases for my adolescence while my father stayed at home.

This structure, the dynamic, and my own identity formed my earliest approach to interpretation.

Many children, when pushed on the playground or are at odds with their newly-minted BFF, run to their parents seeking to purge emotions and receive a calming, endorsing response. "They did what? I'm so sorry. That's not right."  It elicits early validation for a young child with vague grasps of emotions, concepts, and an even vaguer sense of seriousness or time. From the start, my mother's reflex in these situations was always to pause. I was interrupted in my vents to pause and think about - to process - the contributing events. At age 4, I was taught to think about the other side of the story. Did they mean to push me? Did I think they were happy to have done that? Did they feel bad for not sharing? Did I think they were going through something? While less comforting as a child, it taught me to think, observe, perceive, and act differently from a young age. Most importantly, it seeded an early interest in behavior and how ethereal interconnectivity between people, cultures, and systems is.

Over time, this grew with an interest in animals, developing into my university track. From my first thesis in 7th grade, I put my flag firmly into the ground to learn more about cognition, which found its way to introducing me to my first true love, Ethology.

#TAKEAWAYS

While I can't hold a candle to scientists today, and don't formally walking around declaring myself a scientist - I have been out of practice for far too long and have to much respect for the professionals and fields to claim to be a part of something where those practicing worked so hard and studied for so long to be where they are, my inclusion of /Trained Scientist is to offer insight into my background, highlight my applicable skills of which may be advantageous, and share my interest. You don't have to be a hardened science professional to incorporate its methods and practices into any facet of life or work.
