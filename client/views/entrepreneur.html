I first heard of social entrepreneur in 2015 while serving on the leadership board with She's the First. The organization was looking inward to at its efficiences, and I had the fortune to start dialogue with a brilliant woman named Tara Abrahams during this process. She told me that my aspirations made her think me a social entrepreneur, and it all clicked. In fact, the 2nd definition provided below was how she described it to me.

SOCIAL ENTREPRENEUR /ˈsōSHəl / ˌäntrəprəˈnər/

noun

  1. A person who establishes an enterprise with the aim of solving social problems or effecting social change.
  2. Person responding to the rate at which social change is happening.

#BACKGROUND

The consummate over-committer, I have always found myself in numerous groups, organizations, and clubs from an early age. From serving on county-wide youth advisory board for the Department of Education at 15 to founding a student organization at University at 20 to serving on a leadership board for the latter organization at 24.

Much to the ire of my friends over the years, working & making an impact has been something I couldn't shake. As I've matured and begun my career, my past meandering through different spaces has lent me with quite a pronounced perspective. Impact is a lense I see through; it's a motivation, and it is innate.

The following are relevant examples of non-profit organizations I have worked or volunteered with to better give an idea of why I think myself a social entrepreneur. Each of these experiences will have marked skills or takeaways following them.
