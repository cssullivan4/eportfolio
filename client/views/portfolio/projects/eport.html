#ePORTFOLIO

>>> IDEA & PURPOSE

The purpose of this is site to present past works and experiences that shed more light on my background, my character, and ultimately what I am able to contribute as a member on any professional team.
The idea is a more interative take on the resume, where through the filtered learn more view, my resume is organized by interests, then displayed as "cards" with which to pull & learn. I let the content guide the brand, so pages vary by the interests, the largest departure being the "Emerging Designer" funnel.

>>> APPROACH

The challenge with building an eportfolio is the same for everyone: narrowing scope and focus on the relevant. I've had several "eportfolios" since 2003, but none as serious, comprehensive, and bootleg as this. While I took indulgence, the approach remained the same: "present work & experiences that show you are as a candidate, what shaped you, and what is within your wheelhouse as an employee."

>>> BUILD

Beginning first on paper, the build was done so in a class-heavy manner using Bootstrap. This allowed for a flexible, ever-changing ( and trust me, has this site changed a lot from even minute code changes) site that meandered according to what the idea necessitated. As the site grew impressively, this method has been determined to not be the best. Architectually, many changes are happening behind-the-scenes, the biggest being the recent Sass conversion.

>>> FEATURES

  • 2 filtered views (focus on work & focus on the story, the person)
  • Different brands according to categories (ex: "Aspiring Social Entrepreneur" vs. "Emerging Designer")
  • Email service
  • Navigation tailored towards 1) filtered view, 2) individual pages, and with 3) sub-navigations
  • Content management on large scale
  • Custom icons
  • Mobile first responsiveness
  • Photo galleries

>>> TECHNOLOGIES

  • AngularJS, ExpressJS, jQuery, NodeJS, Bootstrap, Sass, Amazon Web Services, Figma

>>> FUTURE UPDATES

  • Convert to Sass
  • Update to Angular 4
  • Update to Bootstrap 4 or remove entirely
  • Add large-scale media to /Emerging Designer
  • Compress all images
  • Add snappable, draggable components
  • Address color brands
  • Institute CRUD capabilities
  • Finish adding images to cards
  • More finite response to tablet screens
  • Build out brand, maker, biography pages and see work filter
  • Address any weaknesses with web accessibility
